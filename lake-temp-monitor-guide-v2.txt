1.  Audit Summary — What Changed and Why
This guide was audited against the live ESPHome documentation. The firmware YAML in the original contained several
errors that would have caused compile failures. All have been corrected in Section 7. The shopping list has also been
simplified. Here is a full account of every change.
Firmware Errors Fixed (would have caused compile failures)
#What Was WrongWhat It Should Be
1nss_pin: used for chip selectcs_pin: is the correct ESPHome key
2hw_version: missing entirelyRequired field — must be hw_version: sx1262
3modulation: missing entirelyRequired field — must be modulation: LORA
4tx_power: 17 (wrong key, wrong range)Correct key is pa_power: (integer, max 22 for SX1262)
5coding_rate: "4/5" (string, invalid)Must use enum: CR_4_5  (or CR_4_6, CR_4_7, CR_4_8)
6tcxo_voltage / tcxo_delay missingHeltec V3 has TCXO on DIO3 — omitting causes radio instability
7esphome: block used platform: / board: inlineModern ESPHome uses a separate esp32: component block
8Byte-packing Fahrenheit as uint8_t (max 255, decimal math broken)Pack Celsius as signed int16_t × 10; convert to °F on gateway
9packet_transport: used fabricated on_receive: / send_packet() APIReal API: use sx126x on_packet: trigger and sx126x.send_packet: action
Hardware / Shopping List Changes
ChangeReason
Reduced from 2x 18650 (parallel) to 1x 18650At your duty cycle (~0.5mA avg), one 3000mAh cell with solar is essentially inexhaustible. The parallel pack added wiring complexity for zero practical benefit.
Removed 20-pack single holders + JST crimp kitOverkill with a single cell. Replaced with one pre-wired holder with JST 1.25mm lead already attached — plug and play.
Reduced Samsung 30Q from 4-pack to 2-packOne for the node, one spare. Saves ~$10.
GPIO37 battery ADC marked as 'verify first'Pin varies between Heltec V3 hardware revisions. Check your board's schematic before trusting it.
n Everything else in the original guide was verified correct: board selection, SPI GPIO pins, DS18B20 wiring, 1-Wire bus
topology, solar panel choice, enclosure, deployment strategy, and Home Assistant integration.

4.  Hardware Assembly — Dock Node
Step 1: Extend the DS18B20 probe cables
The  probes  come  with  ~3m  (10ft)  of  cable.  Cut  your  22  AWG  3-conductor  wire  to  reach  from  the  box  location  to  the
water entry point, adding 15% slack. Strip ends and solder to the probe wires:
• Red (VCC) → Red conductor
• Black (GND) → Black conductor
• Yellow/White (DATA) → remaining conductor
n Waterproof every joint. Slide heat-shrink over each conductor BEFORE soldering, then shrink after. Add a larger piece of
heat-shrink over all three combined. This junction must be 100% waterproof.
Step 2: Identify DS18B20 addresses
Every  DS18B20  has  a  unique  64-bit  address.  You  need  both  to  assign  each  probe  in  ESPHome.  Flash  the  address
scanner YAML from Section 7, open the ESPHome log, connect ONE probe at a time, and record each address. Label
them WATER and AIR with tape.
Step 3: Wire probes to Heltec V3
ConnectionHeltec V3 PinNotes
DS18B20 VCC (both probes)3.3VBoth red wires to same 3.3V pin
DS18B20 GND (both probes)GNDBoth black wires to same GND
DS18B20 DATA (both probes)GPIO5Both data wires share this pin — 1-Wire bus
4.7 kΩ pull-up resistorBetween GPIO5 and 3.3VOne resistor shared by both probes
Solar panel +SOLAR+ (JST 1.25)Via adapted SH1.25 connector
Solar panel −SOLAR− (JST 1.25)Via adapted SH1.25 connector
18650 battery +BAT+ (JST 1.25)Pre-wired holder plugs directly in
18650 battery −BAT− (JST 1.25)Verify polarity with multimeter first!
n Measure battery polarity with a multimeter before inserting cells. Many holders have reversed polarity wiring. Plugging in
backwards will damage the board.
Step 4: Assemble enclosure
• Drill PG7 cable gland holes in the BOTTOM only — never top or sides
• Thread probe cable through gland #1 and tighten once positioned
• Thread solar cable through gland #2 and tighten
• Mount Heltec V3 inside on the mounting plate with foam tape or M2 standoffs
• Connect all wires, insert battery, snap the LoRa antenna to the U.FL connector

5.  Hardware Assembly — Home Gateway
The gateway is simple — it lives inside your home, plugged into USB power. No battery, no solar, no weatherproofing.
• Take the second Heltec V3 board
• Attach the included 915 MHz LoRa antenna to the U.FL connector
• Connect via USB-C to any 5V USB adapter
• Position near a window facing the direction of the lake for best receive sensitivity
n Assign a static DHCP reservation for the gateway in UniFi: Network → Clients → find gateway → Settings → Fixed IP.
Without this, ESPHome loses the device after a DHCP lease expires.

6.  Outdoor Deployment & Camouflage
Mount location criteria
• South-facing sky exposure for the solar panel (diffuse light is fine)
• At least 3–4 feet off the ground — height dramatically improves LoRa range
• On the opposite side of the trunk from any trail or path
• Within 75 feet of the water's edge to keep the probe cable run simple
Camouflage the box
• Lightly sand the ABS box with 120-grit sandpaper to create tooth for paint
• Apply 2 thin coats: dark green base, brown over-spray. Cure 24 hours
• Optional: attach strips of camo burlap or twine to break up the outline
The Official-Looking Label — Most Effective Theft Deterrent
Print this on white card stock and laminate it. Attach to the front of the box with weatherproof adhesive:
ENVIRONMENTAL MONITORING STATION
nnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnnn
Lake Water Quality Program
This device monitors water and air temperature
for recreational water quality assessment.
DO NOT DISTURB — SENSOR IN USE
Report tampering: Parks & Recreation Dept.
Device ID: LWQ-2024-0042
n Fishermen and hikers almost universally assume a labeled box is official equipment and leave it alone. The 'Parks &
Recreation Dept.' phrasing is particularly effective.
Running the probe cable
• Lay cable along the base of vegetation, following natural contours
• Use landscape staples every 2–3 feet to pin it to the ground
• Cover with leaf litter or loose soil along the run
• At the water's edge, screw a stainless eye bolt into a submerged root or stable log
• Thread the probe through the eye bolt and tie off — hang the probe 12–18 inches below surface
Solar panel mounting options
• Option A (preferred): Mount at 30–45 degrees facing south on the box lid or nearby branch. Even partial sky
exposure works at your low duty cycle.
• Option B (dense canopy): Mount horizontally on top of the box facing straight up. Diffuse sky light through thin
canopy is enough. Also hardest to spot from the ground.
n Never place leaf litter ON the solar cells — only around the frame. Any direct shading significantly reduces output.

7.  Software Setup — Corrected ESPHome Firmware
n All YAML in this section has been corrected from v1 of this guide. Key fixes: cs_pin (not nss_pin), hw_version and
modulation are now present, pa_power replaces tx_power, coding_rate uses correct enum format, tcxo settings added,
esp32: is a separate block, and temperature byte-packing uses signed int16_t in Celsius.
Prerequisites
• Home Assistant installed and running
• ESPHome add-on installed (HA → Settings → Add-ons → ESPHome)
• ESPHome version 2025.7.0 or newer
• Both Heltec V3 boards connected via USB-C for first flash
Step 1: Address Scanner
Flash  this  to  the  dock  node  first  with  both  probes  connected.  Open  the  ESPHome  log  and  record  both  DS18B20
addresses — one at a time, labeling each WATER or AIR.
esphome:
  name: address-scanner
esp32:
  board: heltec_wifi_lora_32_V3
  framework:
    type: arduino
logger:
  level: DEBUG
one_wire:
  - platform: gpio
    pin: GPIO5
dallas_temp:
  update_interval: 5s
  # After boot, check logs for lines like:
  # [D][dallas_temp]: Found dallas device '0x28FF...' on bus 0
  # Record BOTH addresses, label them WATER and AIR
Step 2: Dock Node Firmware
Replace both DS18B20 addresses with your own from Step 1:

  friendly_name: Lake Dock Node

OTA Firmware Updates
The gateway updates over-the-air from the ESPHome add-on — no physical access needed. To update the dock node
(no WiFi configured):
• Add a temporary wifi: block to the dock node YAML matching your phone hotspot SSID/password
• Walk close to the deployment site and enable the hotspot
• Trigger OTA from ESPHome in HA
• Remove the wifi: block and re-flash afterward
n At ~5% duty cycle (15 sec awake per 5 min), the dock node draws roughly 0.5mA average. A 3000mAh cell with even
modest solar input will run indefinitely. Annual cell swaps are a precaution, not a requirement.
